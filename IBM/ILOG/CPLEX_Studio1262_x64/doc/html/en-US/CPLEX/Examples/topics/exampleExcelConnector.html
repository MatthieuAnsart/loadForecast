Excel examples

These models in spreadsheets use the CPLEX connector for Excel.

The following examples are delivered with CPLEX in IBM ILOG CPLEX Optimization Studio.

blend.xls is a blending problem; the model blends four sources (pure metal, raw materials, scrap, and ingots) to produce an alloy.
diet.xls implements a variation on Dantzig's familiar diet problem; the model can be generated by columns (add foods to the diet) or by rows (add requirements to the diet).
facility.xls solves a facility location problem.
fixcost1.xls solves a production planning problem with fixed costs.
foodmanufact.xls implements a solution to the food production planning problem, well known from the modeling textbook of H. P. Williams; this implementation uses indicator constraints and semi-continuous variables, illustrating the linearization facilities of CPLEX.
indefqpex1.xls solves an indefinite quadratic program (nonPSD QP).
inout1.xls solves a production planning problem aiming to minimize costs where some products can be produced internally and others may be purchased from outside sources.
mipex1.xls solves a basic mixed integer programming (MIP) problem.
mipqex1.xls solves a mixed integer quadratic programming (MIQP) example. That is, the objective function includes a quadratic term, and the model includes integer constraints.
rates.xls solves a problem about generators in a power plant that must meet demand at minimal cost. The model represents the generators as the truth values (that is, true or false, one or zero) of relations, such as less than or equal to, greater than or equal to, and equality.
steel.xls solves the model known as steelT.mod in the AMPL book by Fourer, Gay, and Kernighan.
